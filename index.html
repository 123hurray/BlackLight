Welcome to Black.Lighting

The project home page of BlackLight, a famous Sina Weibo client for Android.

Why another?

Back to 2014...

A High 1 student began to use Weibo, a Chinese clone of Twitter.

Unfortunately, the only phone he had was a super slow MI-ONE.

And that was why all these had begun.

In 2014, Sina Weibo clients on Android were...

  • Some sticky
  • Much heavyweight
  • Sketchy-featured (3rd party clients)

But then he found Weiciyuan on GitHub, and it was

  • Silky-smooth
  • Such lightweight
  • Full-featured & opensource

Weiciyuan is now dead.

Actually, there haven't been any practical updates since summer 2014.

The boy did not give up his pursuit for a full-featured and smooth Weibo client.

As he thought he had some skills in programming,

he set up a project called BlackLight,

whose name came from the way Weiciyuan was granted full permission to Sina Weibo, "Black Magic".

Long long journey...

To the first release.

As is mentioned, the boy "thought" he had some skills in programming.

In fact, the only programming experience he had

was learning EPL(A Chinese clone of Visual Basic) in his primary school years.

He had little experience in Java, nor in software engineering.

Had he thought about these, he would not have even adopt the idea about setting up the project.

But it was already set up.

Things should not be given up at the beginning.

Here he began.

Learn java while writing BlackLight.

Write BlackLight while learning Java.

Anyway, the infrastructure of the project was finished within two months.

He was full of some kinds of joy towards his "intelligence" in programming.

But...

At that time, BlackLight was

  • Poorly designed
  • Sticky
  • Full of messy code

Bravehearts

Though BlackLight was so alpha-staged by then,

there was more than one brave young man

who joined the test group of BlackLight and gave the boy their precious appreciations.

BlackLight would not have gone through all the difficulties without them.

Every man dies, not every man really lives.

The future

BlackLight is dead?

Time flows like a river...

BlackLight has improved a lot

with help from the team and a lot of contributors.

However, the base of the code, the "infrastructure",

was still unchanged due to Peter's busy school life.

It has been more and more obsolete over time.

Peter has thought of refactoring the code base, but it is far too difficult for now.

It is planned that BlackLight should be rewritten from scratch to overcome a lot of limits.
